# Topical Map for Grounded & Stirred

Planned scale: **260 pages** across coffee and cocktail clusters.

## Editorial Rules
| Principle | Rule |
| --- | --- |
| Originality over volume | Ship fewer pages if needed, but every page must answer a sharper question or provide a more tested angle than generic AI content. |
| Three distinctive insights minimum | Every article brief must force at least 3 non-generic insights from tasting notes, side-by-side tests, workflow logs, or service observations. |
| Programmatic with guardrails | No template page should launch unless it can show real curation, useful comparison logic, and a meaningful related-article set. |
| Human verification where the cup or glass matters | Recipes, tasting notes, gear performance claims, historical assertions, and safety guidance require firsthand testing or expert review before publish. |
| Maintainability first | Use a small number of durable clusters and reusable templates instead of exploding taxonomy depth too early. |

| Spirit-forward cocktails | Syrups, infusions, foams, garnishes | Component pages should be linked only when they materially improve the featured drink and not as random tangents. |
| Highballs and spritzes | Seasonal cocktails | Use season and occasion pages as discovery hubs for lighter drinks with clear service contexts. |
| Dessert cocktails | Coffee cluster and flavor pairing guides | Cross-link coffee dessert cocktails into coffee flavor-note and after-dinner content where sensory overlap exists. |
| Programmatic pages | Pillars and tested articles | Programmatic pages must mainly link down into tested articles and up into pillar pages, never loop thin pages to each other. |
