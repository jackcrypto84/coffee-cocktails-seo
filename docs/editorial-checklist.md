# Editorial Checklist

## Before publishing
- Verify every factual claim that is not common operational knowledge.
- Remove or rewrite anything that reads like generic filler.
- Confirm the page has a clear search intent and a real reader payoff.
- Check that the title, introduction, and metadata all describe the same promise.

## For coffee articles
- Confirm brew ratios, timing ranges, and gear-specific advice were tested or clearly marked as generalized guidance.
- Do not invent tasting notes, origin stories, processing details, or scientific explanations.
- Make sure troubleshooting advice prioritizes the highest-impact variables first.

## For cocktail articles
- Confirm recipe structure, dilution logic, and technique notes were tested or explicitly marked for review.
- Do not invent historical origin stories, bar awards, or claims about canonical status.
- If a technique touches safety or food handling, require manual review before publishing.

## Monetization quality bar
- Featured product blocks must solve a specific problem in the article.
- Comparison blocks must explain who each option is best for and why.
- Never let affiliate copy overwhelm the editorial usefulness of the page.

## SEO quality bar
- Add at least three internal links that genuinely help the reader continue.
- Include FAQ only when it answers real follow-up questions.
- Confirm schema markup matches the visible content.
- Avoid programmatic pages that have no unique editorial angle or no meaningful item set.
