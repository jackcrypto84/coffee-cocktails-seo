# Content Review Checklist

## Draft strength
- Does the draft clearly solve the reader's real problem?
- Does it follow the approved brief rather than drifting into generic advice?
- Does it contain at least 3 distinctive insights that generic AI content would likely miss?

## Credibility and sourcing
- Are all unsupported claims still marked with `[VERIFY FACT]` or `[CITE SOURCE]`?
- Are all tasting, brewing, batching, or substitution claims either verified or marked with `[ADD FIRSTHAND TEST NOTE]`?
- Are product references accurate and appropriately cautious?

## Specificity and usefulness
- Does each main section tell the reader what to change, notice, or avoid?
- Are tradeoffs and edge cases addressed where they matter?
- Are substitutions practical for a home setup?

## Style
- Does the voice sound like a knowledgeable enthusiast instead of a hype-driven affiliate page?
- Is the writing concise and vivid without being overwritten?
- Are fake personal stories absent unless explicitly marked for human editing?

## Media and linking
- Is the hero image specific to the actual recipe rather than a generic drink stand-in?
- Do glassware, garnish, texture, color, ice, and layering cues match the visible recipe?
- Is the visual metadata complete enough to produce a believable photorealistic image?
- Are internal links helpful and specific rather than decorative?
